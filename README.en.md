# No Rules Rules Skill

A ready-to-use AI skill pack — 10 composable management modules distilled from Netflix's culture of freedom and responsibility.

This is not a book summary page. The book is the source; these skills are the product.

## What Problems Do These Skills Solve

- Deciding whether to keep "good enough" team members who aren't truly critical
- Building a feedback culture without turning it into emotional venting
- Reducing approvals without losing accountability
- Deciding who makes the final call in high-autonomy teams
- Adapting direct feedback across cultural boundaries

## What Was Distilled

10 triple-verified skills extracted from *No Rules Rules*, covering four dimensions:

Each skill passed triple verification — only methodologies with genuine cross-scenario applicability, predictive power, and uniqueness made it into the final deliverable.

## Effect Examples

### Example 1: Should You Keep a "Pretty Good" Team Member?

**User Question**

"I have someone on the team who isn't bad, but also isn't a key player. Should I keep them?"

**Skill Judgment**

- Trigger `keeper-test` — is this someone you would actively fight to keep?
- If the question expands to team quality, follow up with `talent-density`
- If compensation strategy comes into play, add `elite-compensation`

**Response Example**

> Don't first ask "have they done anything wrong?" — ask a harder question: if they resigned tomorrow, would you genuinely fight to keep them? If the answer is no, the issue usually isn't whether they meet the bar, but whether they deserve to keep occupying that seat. Then look at talent density. A medium performer looks fine in an average team, but in a high-density team they drag down feedback speed, judgment quality, and execution standards. If you already know you wouldn't fight to retain them, don't drag it out with vague evaluations.

### Example 2: Before You Cut the Rules

**User Question**

"Our company has too many approvals. Everyone complains about being slow. Should I just remove the rules?"

**Skill Judgment**

- Trigger `context-not-control` — is the real problem missing context?
- Follow up with `eliminate-controls` — which rules can be replaced by principles?
- If you need to decide who makes the final call, add `informed-captain`

**Response Example**

> Don't start from "cut the rules" — first check whether the team has enough shared context. Without context, removing approvals just moves the chaos earlier. You need to confirm three things first: does everyone have enough information, does everyone understand the same goal, and does everyone know the decision boundaries? Only when all three are in place does it make sense to replace rules with principles and shift from pre-approval to context-setting plus retrospective review. If those three aren't in place, you're not cutting bureaucracy — you're cutting the last error-catching mechanism.

## How These Skills Were Generated

These skills were produced using the **cangjie-skill** RIA-TV++ pipeline, a six-stage process:

1. **Whole-Book Comprehension (Adler Analysis)** — Structural, interpretive, critical, and applicability analysis of the entire book, producing `BOOK_OVERVIEW.md`
2. **Parallel Extraction** — Five specialized extractors (frameworks, principles, cases, counter-examples, glossary) run simultaneously to pull candidate units from the source text
3. **Triple Verification** — Each candidate must pass three checks: at least 2 independent supporting passages in the book (cross-domain), ability to answer a novel question not explicitly addressed (predictive power), and non-commonsense uniqueness. Pass rate is typically 25-50%
4. **RIA++ Construction** — Verified content is structured into six dimensions: R (original quote) / I (own-words reconstruction) / A1 (book cases) / A2 (future trigger scenarios) / E (executable steps) / B (boundaries & blind spots)
5. **Zettelkasten Linking** — Dependency, contrast, and composition relationships between skills are identified, producing `INDEX.md` with a reference graph
6. **Pressure Testing** — Test prompts including bait questions are designed for each skill; failures go back to Stage 4 for full reconstruction

Every `SKILL.md` is a real output of this process. `candidates/` and `rejected/` directories are preserved as a complete audit trail.
